Comparison of four Generalized Linear Mixed Models (GLMM)

Data of 16 conditions was plotted using a GLMM. Four models with increasing complexity (main effects only, main effects with quadratic terms, main effects with two-way interaction terms, and main effects with quadratic terms and two-way interaction terms. Models were compared using expected behavior, Nakagawa's R², and Root Mean Square Error (RMSE). The ideal model reacts as expected, has a high R², and a low RMSE.)

Chosen GLMM

Once the models have been fitted with 16 training conditions and validated with 8 additional conditions, the model including main effects only was chosen as the model best displaying the data, while keeping low complexity. The complete dataset, from 16 training conditions and 8 validation conditions, was refitted to enhance the model.

Other GLMM performing similarly

Upon refitting the model for all four models, both the model with main effects and quadratic terms and the model with main effects and two-way interaction terms, performed well. The model including both main effects, quadratic terms, and two-way interaction terms showed the highest R², but also an RMSE almost double as large compared to the other models.
